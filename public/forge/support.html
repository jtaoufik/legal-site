FAQ

How does the push-up counter work?

Forge uses Apple's on-device Vision framework to detect the angle of your elbows. When both elbows go from fully extended (≥155°) → bent (≤95°) → extended again, that counts as one rep. No video is ever recorded, uploaded, or stored.

The counter isn't detecting my reps. What now?

Prop your phone so the front camera sees your whole body — head, shoulders, elbows, wrists. The visibility pill at the top right tells you how clearly Forge can see you. Aim for at least 80%. Even lighting helps too.

Can I block other apps yet?

Real iOS app-blocking requires Apple's Family Controls entitlement, which is granted by application (1–4 weeks). Until then Forge runs in "honor mode": it sends a time-sensitive notification when your session ends, and asks you to close the gated app yourself.

How is the push-up cost calculated?

By default: 2 push-ups per minute for the first 10 minutes, then 0.5 push-ups per minute beyond that. So 10 min = 20 push-ups, 30 min = 30 push-ups, 60 min = 35 push-ups. You can tune both rates in Schedule → Push-up formula.

What's the free allowance?

Each day-of-week has a free minute allowance — sessions within that window cost zero push-ups. Once it's used up for the day, every additional minute costs push-ups. Configure per-day in the Schedule tab.

Why does the camera need access?

To count push-ups. Frames stay in memory only and are discarded immediately. You can revoke camera access any time in iOS Settings → Forge → Camera.

Delete my data?

All Forge data lives on your device. Delete the app to remove everything. There's no cloud copy.
