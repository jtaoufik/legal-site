Frequently asked questions

What does WakeWise do that the iPhone Clock app doesn't?
The Clock app fires exactly at the time you set, no matter what stage of sleep you're in — that's why some mornings feel brutal. WakeWise watches your heart rate and motion through Apple Watch and fires up to 30 minutes earlier, but only when you're in light sleep. Same wake-up time ceiling, far gentler exit.
Do I have to tap "Start tracking" before bed?
No. WakeWise auto-starts sleep tracking whenever an enabled alarm is within the next 10 hours. Just wear your Watch and go to sleep.
Does WakeWise need internet?
No. The app works entirely offline. Nothing is sent to any server.
Does it require an Apple Watch?
Yes — the smart-wake feature reads heart rate and motion from your Apple Watch. Without a Watch the iPhone app still fires the alarm at the ceiling time (like a regular alarm) but the "wake at the light-sleep moment" magic is Watch-only.
What happens if I'm in deep sleep when the window opens?
WakeWise waits. If you don't reach light sleep before the ceiling time, the alarm fires at the ceiling time. You will never oversleep.
How is sleep stage detected?
Two signals: heart rate (drops in deep sleep, variable in REM) and wrist motion (still in deep, twitchy in REM, large in awake). The combined signal classifies you into Awake / Light / Deep every 30 seconds. Heart rate access uses Apple HealthKit; you can revoke it any time.
Does the alarm bypass Do Not Disturb / Sleep / Focus modes?
WakeWise notifications are flagged as time-sensitive, which bypasses Focus modes automatically. iOS Critical Alerts (which bypass DND silenced ringer) require an extra Apple entitlement that we're applying for — it will arrive in a free update.
Can I add alarms from the Apple Watch?
Yes. The Watch shows the same alarm list as the iPhone — add, edit, toggle, delete from either device and the change syncs both ways.
Is there a subscription?
No subscription, ever. WakeWise is a one-time purchase on the App Store. Every future update is free.
Where is my data?
On your iPhone and Apple Watch only. Heart rate, sleep stage, alarm history — none of it leaves your devices. We don't operate any servers. See the privacy policy for details.
